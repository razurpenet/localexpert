# Handby — Pre-Launch Demand Generation Plan
> Date: 2026-03-14
> Goal: Create desire, urgency, and anticipation for Handby BEFORE launch
> Target: Both providers AND customers in Newham (primary) + wider UK (awareness)

---

## THE CORE STRATEGY: BUILD IN PUBLIC + WAITLIST SCARCITY

The entire pre-launch strategy revolves around one principle: **make people feel like they're missing out if they don't get early access.**

Handby isn't "another Checkatrade." It's the first platform built *after* the Border Security Act, designed for the 2026 regulatory reality. That's the story.

---

## PHASE 1 — BRAND IDENTITY & NARRATIVE (Week 1–2)

### 1.1 Craft the Origin Story

People don't share apps. People share stories. Your pre-launch content needs ONE clear narrative:

> **"We saw tradespeople losing jobs because they couldn't navigate UK bureaucracy. We saw customers scared of £60,000 fines for hiring the wrong person. So we built Handby — the first marketplace where every provider is verified, insured, and legally checked. No guesswork."**

This story works for:
- **Providers:** "Someone finally gets my problem"
- **Customers:** "Finally, I don't have to gamble on a Facebook recommendation"
- **Press:** "New startup tackles post-Brexit compliance gap in trades"
- **Immigrant communities:** "Someone is building something that actually helps us"

### 1.2 Visual Identity for Pre-Launch

Before you post anything, lock in:
- A clean, recognisable logo (you likely have this)
- A consistent colour palette across all social
- A "Coming Soon" landing page at handby.uk with:
  - The story (3 sentences max)
  - A waitlist signup (email + postcode)
  - "Are you a provider?" / "Are you a customer?" toggle
  - A countdown or "launching [month]" banner
  - Social proof: "Join 200+ providers already signed up" (update this live)

### 1.3 Social Accounts — Claim & Set Up

## PHASE 2 — WAITLIST & ANTICIPATION ENGINE (Week 2–4)

### 2.1 The Waitlist — Your Most Powerful Tool

The waitlist isn't just an email list. It's a **psychological commitment device.**

**Waitlist landing page must include:**
- "Be first in your area" — geographic scarcity
- "Founding Providers get free listing for 12 months" — financial incentive
- "Founding Customers get £10 credit on first job" — immediate value
- A progress bar: "347 people in Newham have joined" — social proof + FOMO
- A referral mechanism: "Move up the waitlist — share your link, get priority access"

**Referral waitlist tool options:**
- Viral Loops (£29/month) — built for this exact use case
- Waitlist.me or Gleam.io
- Or build a simple one: unique referral code per signup, track referrals, rank position

**The referral mechanic:**
```
Sign up → Get a unique link → Share with friends
Each referral = move up 10 spots
Top 50 = VIP early access + "Founding Member" badge
Top 10 = Personal onboarding call with the founder
```

This turns every signup into a micro-marketer.

### 2.2 Provider-Specific Waitlist Messaging

Providers are the supply side. Without them, there's no marketplace. They need different messaging:

**For general UK providers:**
> "Checkatrade charges £500/year before you get a single job. Handby is free for your first 12 months. Join the waitlist — spots are limited."

**For immigrant/diaspora providers:**
> "You have the skills. We handle the UK paperwork. RTW verification, insurance guidance, multilingual profiles — all built in. Join the waitlist."

**For African diaspora providers (via community channels):**
> "Stop relying on WhatsApp groups to find clients. Get a professional profile, real reviews, and customers who can actually find you. Founding providers get 12 months free."

### 2.3 Customer-Specific Waitlist Messaging

**For homeowners:**
> "Tired of dodgy reviews on Facebook Marketplace? Every Handby provider is Right-to-Work verified, insured, and reviewed by real customers. Be first to try it."

**For renters:**
> "Need a plumber but don't know who to trust? Handby finds you verified local pros in minutes. Join the waitlist — launching in [area] soon."

**For the diaspora community:**
> "Find your braider, caterer, tailor, or barber — no more scrolling WhatsApp groups. Real profiles. Real reviews. Real people in your community."

## PHASE 3 — CONTENT STRATEGY: BUILD DESIRE BEFORE THE PRODUCT (Week 2–8)

### 3.1 Content Pillars (Pre-Launch)

You're not selling an app yet. You're selling a **movement**. Every piece of content should make people think: "I need this."

**Pillar 1: Provider Stories (60% of content)**
These are your most powerful asset. Real people, real skills, real stories.

Content ideas:
- "Meet [Name], a master carpenter from [country] who's been doing cash jobs for 3 years because he couldn't get on Checkatrade" — video interview (60 sec)
- "She braids hair for 200 clients a month but has zero online presence" — photo carousel
- "This electrician passed his 18th Edition but can't afford £500 to list on Checkatrade" — story post
- "Day in the life of a Newham handyman" — TikTok/Reel
- Before & after photos of provider work (with permission) — Instagram carousel

**Why this works:** Customers see the quality. Providers see themselves represented. Community members share because it's *their people*.

**Pillar 2: Pain Point Content (25% of content)**
Make the problem feel urgent so the solution feels necessary.

Content ideas:
- "35% of Brits struggled to find a tradesperson last year. Here's why." — infographic
- "The £60,000 fine you didn't know about" — explainer about Border Security Act + RTW
- "WhatsApp groups vs verified platforms: what you're risking" — comparison post
- "How much should a plumber cost in [area]?" — price guide (SEO gold)
- "5 red flags when hiring a tradesperson" — value content that positions Handby as the safe alternative
- "Awaab's Law starts May 2026: what landlords need to know" — targets B2B audience

**Pillar 3: Behind the Scenes / Build in Public (15% of content)**
Let people into the journey. Founders building something > faceless corporation.

Content ideas:
- "We just hit 100 providers on the waitlist" — milestone celebration
- "Designing the provider profile page — what do you think?" — screenshot + poll
- "We visited Peckham market to talk to traders about what they need" — authentic founder content
- "Our first provider just uploaded their portfolio — look at this work" — share (with permission)
- Weekly "build update" — what was built, what's next, subscriber count

### 3.3 Video Content — The Viral Play

Short-form video is the highest-leverage pre-launch content. Prioritise this.

**Video formats that work:**
1. **"I'm building an app and here's why"** — founder talking to camera, 60 seconds, authentic. Post on TikTok + Instagram Reels. This format goes viral when the story is compelling.
2. **Provider showcase** — film a provider doing their craft. No script. Just skill. Caption: "This is [Name]. They're one of the first providers on Handby. [Service] in [Area]."
3. **Problem/solution hook** — "Did you know you can be fined £60,000 for hiring someone without the right to work? [pause] That's why we built Handby."
4. **Street interviews** — "How do you find a tradesperson?" Ask real people in Newham. Their answers will highlight the pain.
5. **Transformation stories** — "Before Handby: scrolling WhatsApp groups for hours. After Handby: verified pro booked in 3 minutes."

**Production quality:** Use a smartphone. Authentic > polished. Add captions (80% watch without sound).

## PHASE 4 — COMMUNITY SEEDING (Week 2–8)

### 4.1 Facebook & WhatsApp Group Infiltration (Not Spam — Value)

This is your highest-ROI pre-launch channel. The communities already exist. You need to join them authentically.

**Target groups (join personally as the founder, NOT as a brand page):**

General:
- "[City] Residents / Community" groups
- "[City] Mums / Parents" groups
- "Tradespeople UK", "Self-Employed UK"
- "UK Sole Traders" groups

Diaspora-specific:
- "Nigerians in London", "Ghanaians in Birmingham", "Africans in Manchester"
- "Polacy w [City]" (Poles in [City])
- "Romanians in East London", "[Nationality] in UK" groups
- Cultural-specific: "Naija Brides UK", "African Caterers UK", "Afro Hair UK"

**Engagement strategy (DO NOT SPAM):**
1. **Week 1–2:** Join. Observe. Like posts. Comment helpfully. Build presence.
2. **Week 3–4:** Start answering questions. "Looking for a plumber in Newham? I'm actually building a platform for this — would love your input on what features matter most."
3. **Week 5–6:** Share a provider story (with their permission). "Met this amazing carpenter in Stratford yesterday. He's been doing brilliant work for 5 years but relies entirely on word of mouth. We're building something to change that."
4. **Week 7–8:** Soft launch announcement. "We've been building Handby for the last few months based on feedback from this community. Launching next month — founding members get [incentive]. Here's the waitlist link."

**Key rule:** Give value 10 times before you ask for anything once.

### 4.2 WhatsApp Broadcast via Community Leaders

Don't broadcast yourself. Find community leaders who will.

**Who to target:**
- Admins of large WhatsApp groups (50+ members)
- Church/mosque community leaders (RCCG, Winners Chapel, local mosques)
- Community centre managers (Welcome Newham, RenewALL Hub)
- Local business network organisers

**The ask:**
> "I'm building a free platform to help local service providers get found and get paid properly. Would you be open to sharing a short message with your community? Founding providers get 12 months free."

**Give them a pre-written message** (make it easy):
> "Hi everyone! A new app called Handby is launching soon to help local tradespeople, caterers, braiders, cleaners, and more get found by customers in the area. It's free to join for the first 12 months. If you offer any kind of service, you can sign up here: [waitlist link]. They're also looking for feedback — what services do you wish were easier to find?"

### 4.3 Church & Mosque Partnerships

This is unconventional but extremely high-trust for diaspora communities.

**Approach:**
- Contact the pastor/imam/community leader directly
- Offer to present for 2 minutes during announcements
- Bring flyers with QR codes
- Frame it as: "We're helping members of this community turn their skills into proper businesses"

**Target in Newham/East London:**
- RCCG parishes (Redemption Camp network)
- Winners Chapel (multiple London branches)
- Celestial Church (strong Nigerian community)
- East London Mosque (Somali, Bangladeshi community)
- Local Ghanaian churches

## PHASE 5 — PR & PRESS (Week 4–8)

### 5.1 Press Angle — What Makes Handby Newsworthy

Journalists don't care about another marketplace. They care about **stories, trends, and controversy.**

**Angle 1: "The Border Security Act created a £60,000 problem. This startup is solving it."**
- Targets: TechCrunch UK, Sifted, The Register, City AM
- Hook: Regulatory change creates new market

**Angle 2: "The UK has 11.4 million immigrants. Most can't get hired through platforms like Checkatrade. Here's why."**
- Targets: The Guardian, BBC News, Channel 4 News
- Hook: Social impact, immigration, economic inclusion
- CAUTION: Frame as empowerment, not victimhood

**Angle 3: "35% of Brits can't find a tradesperson. A Newham startup says it's because 11 million skilled workers are invisible."**
- Targets: Evening Standard, Time Out London, Newham Recorder
- Hook: Local founder, local problem, local solution

**Angle 4: "Awaab's Law forces landlords to fix damp in 10 days. They have no idea how."**
- Targets: Property industry press (The Negotiator, Landlord Today, Property Wire)
- Hook: B2B angle, regulatory pressure, Handby as the solution

### 5.2 Press Kit

Prepare and host on handby.uk/press:
- Founder headshot + bio (2 sentences)
- Handby logo (various formats)
- 3–5 app screenshots / mockups
- One-page fact sheet: what Handby is, key stats, launch date, waitlist link
- 2–3 provider stories (with permission) — these ARE the story
- Key stat cards: "£15B market", "11.4M immigrants", "£60K fines", "35% can't find a tradesperson"

### 5.3 Journalist Outreach

**Who to contact:**
- Tech reporters at Sifted, TechCrunch, The Verge UK
- Immigration/society reporters at The Guardian, BBC
- Local reporters at Newham Recorder, East London Advertiser
- Property reporters at Landlord Today, The Negotiator
- Diaspora media: The Voice (UK Black newspaper), Ovation International, Bella Naija (Nigerian diaspora)

**How to pitch (keep it SHORT):**
```
Subject: UK startup tackles £60,000 immigration compliance gap in trades

Hi [Name],

I'm building Handby — a marketplace for local tradespeople that handles
Right-to-Work verification, insurance, and UK compliance automatically.

The Border Security Act 2025 creates fines up to £60,000 for platforms
that don't verify providers. We're the first marketplace built after
this legislation, designed for 2026.

We launch in Newham (53.7% non-UK born, most diverse borough in London)
with 100+ providers already on the waitlist.

Happy to chat for 15 minutes if this is interesting for [publication].

[Name]
Founder, Handby
```

### 5.4 Podcast Appearances

Podcasts are underrated for pre-launch. Founders talking authentically for 30–60 minutes builds deep trust.

**Target podcasts:**
- The Diary of a CEO (reach out — startup stories are their format)
- Startup Grind UK
- The Side Hustle Show
- My First Million (if you can get on, it's massive)
- The Property Podcast (landlord/B2B angle)
- Afrobeats & Business (diaspora entrepreneurship)

## THE EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS — WHY THIS WORKS

This plan is designed to trigger five psychological drivers:

1. **Scarcity** — "Founding Provider spots are limited." "First 500 customers get £10 credit." People want what they might miss.

2. **Belonging** — "Join the community." "Your neighbours are already signed up." People want to be part of something.

3. **Identity** — "Built for tradespeople like you." "Your skills deserve a platform." People engage with brands that reflect who they are.

4. **Reciprocity** — "12 months free." "We built this based on YOUR feedback." When you give first, people feel compelled to give back (signups, shares, referrals).

5. **Story** — Nobody shares a feature list. Everybody shares a story about a carpenter from Lagos who couldn't get on Checkatrade but is now fully booked through Handby. **The providers ARE the marketing.**
